About Me

Hello! I'm Kate and I am currently in my fourth year of Software Engineering at the University of Alberta.

My programming journey began in the ninth grade when I went to work with my dad for take-your-kid-to-work-day and made my very first application. From there I took Computer Science classes in high school which allowed me to gain fundamental skills in HTML/CSS, JavaScript and Java. This led me to university where I learned C++ and Python and gained invaluable group work experience.

I have a passion for user experience and strive to develop meaninful experiences for users. I love being creative and taking on a challenge.

Pandemic Simulator | January 2021

Pandemic simulator was inspired by the COVID-19 pandemic. We wanted to hack on a project that could promote awareness for the importance of safety precautions. The project simulates the spread of a disease in a community by having agents navigate the community and periodically visit concentrated areas such as a grocery store

Technovation

January - April 2017

Technovation is an international competition designed to empower women to pursue careers in STEM. The goal of the competition is to develop a mobile application to tackle one of the United Nation's sustainable development goals. Our team focused on quality education and created an app to allow high school students more easily compare the programs offered by different post-secondary institutions.

We conducted market research, developed a realistic business plan and developed the application from scratch using MIT App Inventor. The application was pitched to a live panel of judges and won the award for best pitch.
